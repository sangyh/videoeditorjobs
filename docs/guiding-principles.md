# Guiding Principles

Use this as the product and go-to-market filter for Video Editor Jobs. Anything we build, write, automate, or promote should serve this ICP and reduce these pain points.

## Target Market

The biggest market is early creator businesses that need repeat monthly output.

This includes YouTubers, podcasters, coaches, founders, educators, agencies, and small media businesses that have moved past occasional one-off edits and need a reliable publishing workflow.

The strongest evidence so far points to recurring output demand: Business Insider/YTJobs reported video editor roles were 61% of creator job postings in Q4, and public forum posts repeatedly ask for weekly videos, monthly retainers, long-term editors, turnaround, portfolios, relevant samples, and paid tests.

## Beachhead

The best initial wedge is solo experts, coaches, consultants, educators, and founder-led personal brands turning long-form content into Shorts, Reels, YouTube, and podcast clips.

This beachhead is stronger than broad creator hiring because:

- They have clearer business ROI than hobby creators.
- They usually need recurring work, not one-off edits.
- They are easier to target on LinkedIn, Instagram, YouTube, podcast directories, and coaching communities.
- They often do not need ultra-niche gaming or meme knowledge.
- They need a packaged workflow: podcast or vlog edit, 4-12 clips per month, captions, and posting-ready files.
- Forum evidence shows buyer demand for clean long-form plus short-form repurposing, including coaches and agency clients.

## Beachhead Offer

We match coaches, consultants, and founder-led creators with vetted editors who turn long-form calls, podcasts, and videos into polished weekly clips.

## First ICP

$5k+/month coaches, consultants, founders, educators, and niche experts who are already posting video but are inconsistent.

This ICP gives the business a focused buyer, recurring revenue potential, clear outbound channels, and a practical seller-vetting standard.

## Core Pain Points

### Trust Gap

Buyers are flooded with portfolios and generic DMs. They cannot easily tell which editors are actually good, reliable, relevant to their format, or capable of recurring work.

### Workflow Gap

Buyers need help defining the work before they can hire well: handoff process, project files, revision rules, content calendar, asset organization, reference examples, turnaround, and monthly deliverables.

### Quality Gap

Buyers increasingly reject AI slop, generic jump-cut edits, template captions, and work that looks technically edited but does not improve clarity, taste, retention, or brand trust.

### Sales Burden For Editors

The current application process is very sales-heavy. Buyers say generic DMs will be ignored, require portfolios, ask for rates and relevant niche samples, and sometimes require forms or paid tests. That creates a barrier for editors who are creatively strong but weak at positioning themselves.

## Product Filter

Prefer features, content, and operations that help one of these jobs:

- Help a serious creator describe a recurring editing workflow clearly.
- Help a buyer trust that an editor is relevant, reliable, and fairly priced.
- Help an editor show niche fit without writing a sales pitch from scratch.
- Turn messy DMs into a structured brief, paid test, and recurring engagement.
- Make long-form-to-short-form packages easier to buy, scope, fulfill, and review.

Avoid work that pulls the product toward a generic freelancer marketplace, broad job board, or hobbyist creator forum unless it directly improves the beachhead ICP.
